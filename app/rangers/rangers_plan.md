# Humber River Rangers

## Core Purpose

Humber River Rangers is a volunteer community focused on restoring and protecting the Humber River in Toronto through recurring cleanups, water-health projects, and practical climate action.

This project is designed to help people get outside, build real local relationships, and contribute to environmental outcomes that can be measured over time.

## Page Vision

The Rangers page should feel like a living community hub:
- Mission and participation overview
- Monthly participation network visualization
- Upcoming events and signup flow
- Support options (merch first, donation optional)
- Environmental creator media spotlight
- Inspiration from Jacques Cousteau, David Suzuki, and Jane Goodall
- Regional river connection narrative (Stratford, Toronto, St. Lawrence corridor)

## Participation Network (Primary Visualization)

The map section should be an Eyeo-inspired connection visualization, not just a geographic map.

### Intent

Show how people participate each month and how strong community ties form over time.

### Visual Behavior

- Each participant appears as a node.
- Links between participants represent shared participation (cleanups, projects, support actions).
- Stronger recurring collaboration produces thicker and brighter links.
- Every contributor remains visible, even with light activity, so all participation is recognized.

### Monthly View

- A month selector updates the network for that month.
- Companion metrics should include:
	- active participants
	- new participants
	- returning participants
	- total connection strength
